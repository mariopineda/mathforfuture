  1. A regular polygon has a perimeter of 34.08 cm and a side length of 5.68 cm. How many sides does the polygon have?
  2. One year, a student council sold 856 copies of a school yearbook. Four fifths of the students at the school bought a copy. How many students did not buy a yearbook.
  3. A score of 17 on a math test results in a mark of 68%. What score would give you a mark of 100%?
  4. The area of Nunavut is about $4\fraction{3}{8}$ times the area of the Yukon Territory. Nunavut covers 21% of Canada’s area. What percent of Canada’s area does the Yukon Territory cover?
  5. I am thinking of a number. When you multiply the number by 5 and then add 14, you get the same result as when you add 10 to the number I am thinking of and then multiply the result by 2. What number am I thinking about?

A.

$x = $ number of sides in polygon.

side length $\times$ number of sides $=$ perimeter

$5.68x=34.08$

\[ \begin{array}{rcl} 5.68x & = & 34.08 \\ x & = & \fraction{34.08}{5.68} \\ x & = & 6 \end{array} \]

The polygon has 6 sides (hexagon).
